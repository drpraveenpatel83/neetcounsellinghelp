✅ Eligibility Criteria & Domicile Rules

Only Indian citizens are eligible. OCI/PIO candidates are not eligible for any reservation or fee concessions. Candidates are classified into three domicile categories:

CategoryDetails & Eligibility
KeraliteKerala origin or AIS officers’ children posted in Kerala. Eligible for all government seats, reservation, fee concessions, and private seats.
Non-Keralite I (NK I)Studied in Kerala and parent posted in Govt/Defense in Kerala OR Resident in Kerala for 5 out of 12 years OR Studied from class 8 to 12 in Kerala.
Eligible for: Private self-financing colleges under state merit quota (No communal reservation).
Non-Keralite II (NK II)All other Indian citizens (Other-state candidates).
Eligible for: 15% seats in Private Self-financing Colleges only. No reservation, no fee concession, not eligible for government seats.
NEET QualificationMust qualify NEET UG 2026 (50% in PCB for General, 40% for SC/ST/OBC).

📋 Counselling Process (Step-by-Step)

Kerala AYUSH Counselling is primarily conducted via the CEE KEAM portal.

  • 1
    KEAM Online Registration
    Register through KEAM 2026 on the CEE Kerala portal. Upload necessary nativity/domicile and category documents and pay the application fee.
  • 2
    Submission of NEET Score
    After NTA declares NEET results, candidates must log into the CEE portal to submit their NEET Roll Number, Score, and AIR.
  • 3
    AYUSH Rank List
    CEE will publish the Kerala AYUSH State Rank list based on the submitted NEET scores. NK-II candidates are listed under the 15% private quota.
  • 4
    Choice Filling (Option Entry)
    Fill choices for preferred colleges and courses through the Centralized Allotment Process (CAP). Save and lock your choices.
  • 5
    Allotment & Admission
    Download the allotment memo. Report to the allotted medical college with original documents and pay the requisite fees.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can non-Kerala students apply for Kerala AYUSH Counselling?

Yes, non-Kerala candidates fall under the Non-Keralite II (NK-II) category. They are eligible for the 15% open/management seats in Private Self-financing AYUSH Colleges only. They cannot claim any communal reservation and do not have access to government seats.

Who conducts the Kerala AYUSH Counselling 2026?

The Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE), Kerala conducts the KEAM counselling for all Government, Govt-Aided, and Private AYUSH Colleges in the state.

What is the fee for Govt BAMS Colleges in Kerala?

The annual tuition fee for Government BAMS and BHMS colleges in Kerala generally ranges between highly subsidized ₹5,530 to ₹23,105 per annum.
