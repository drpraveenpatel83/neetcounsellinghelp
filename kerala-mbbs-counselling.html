📌 CEE Kerala conducts the Centralised Allotment Process (CAP) for 85% seats in Government medical colleges and 100% seats in Private medical colleges.

✅ Eligibility & Domicile Rules (Keralite vs Non-Keralite)

Kerala categorizes applicants into three distinct domicile categories.

CategoryCriteria Notes
KeraliteCandidates of Kerala origin. Eligible for all State Merit (Govt) seats, reservation seats, and Private college seats.
Non-Keralite I (NK-I)Candidates not of Kerala origin but fulfilling conditions like 5 years of study in Kerala or parent is Govt. employee in Kerala. Eligible for State Merit (Govt) seats but NOT for communal reservations.
Non-Keralite II (NK-II)Open to all India. Candidates from other states. They are eligible ONLY for the 15% Management/Open Quota seats in Private Medical Colleges.
⚠️ Non-Keralite II (NK-II) candidates cannot claim any communal reservations or fee concessions. They must apply during the KEAM application window to participate.

📋 Counselling Process (Step-by-Step)

  • 1
    KEAM Online Registration
    Register on cee.kerala.gov.in during the KEAM application window. Upload necessary nativity/domicile and category certificates.
  • 2
    Submission of NEET Score
    After NTA declares NEET results, log into the CEE portal to submit your NEET Roll Number, Score, and AIR.
  • 3
    State Merit List
    CEE will publish the Kerala State Medical Rank list based on the submitted NEET scores.
  • 4
    Option Registration (Choice Filling)
    Submit your preferences of colleges via the Centralised Allotment Process (CAP). Save and lock your choices.
  • 5
    Trial & Final Allotment
    A 'Trial Allotment' is published to give an idea of probabilities. After choice modification (if any), the Final Allotment is released. Pay the token fee to CEE and report to the college.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is Kerala an Open State for NEET Counselling?

Yes, Kerala is an Open State. While Government seats are strictly reserved for Keralite students, non-domicile candidates (classified as Non-Keralite II or NK-II) can apply for the 15% Management/Open Quota seats in Private Medical Colleges.

Who conducts the Kerala MBBS Counselling 2026?

The counselling is conducted by the Commissioner for Entrance Examinations (CEE), Kerala through the official portal cee.kerala.gov.in. The process is known as KEAM.

When should I apply for Kerala MBBS counselling?

Unlike most states, CEE Kerala usually opens the KEAM application portal before the NEET exam. Candidates must complete the initial registration early and upload their NEET scores later when results are declared.

What is the fee for Government Medical Colleges in Kerala?

The annual tuition fee for pure Government Medical Colleges in Kerala is highly subsidized, currently around ₹28,950 per year.
